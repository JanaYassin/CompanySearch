SUMMARY
Tesla, Inc. designs, develops, manufactures, leases, and sells electric vehicles, and energy generation and storage systems in the United States, China, and internationally.
The Automotive segment offers electric vehicles, as well as sells automotive regulatory credits; and non-warranty after-sales vehicle, used vehicles, retail merchandise, and vehicle insurance services. It operates in two segments, Automotive, and Energy Generation / Storage
This segment also provides sedans and sport utility vehicles through direct and used vehicle sales, a network of Tesla Superchargers, and in-app upgrades; purchase financing and leasing services; services for electric vehicles through its company-owned service locations and Tesla mobile service technicians; and vehicle limited warranties and extended service plans.
The Energy Generation and Storage segment engages in the design, manufacture, installation, sale, and leasing of solar energy generation and energy storage products, and related services to residential, commercial, and industrial customers and utilities through its website, stores, and galleries, as well as through a network of channel partners; and provision of service and repairs to its energy product customers, including under warranty, as well as various financing options to its solar customers.
The company was formerly known as Tesla Motors, Inc. and changed its name to Tesla, Inc. in February 2017. Tesla, Inc. was incorporated in 2003 and is headquartered in Austin, Texas.

SWOT REPORT
STRENGTH
1. Innovative Products: Tesla is known for its cutting-edge electric vehicles and energy generation and storage systems, which have gained significant popularity in the market.
2. Strong Financial Performance: The company has consistently delivered strong financial results, with a return per asset of 10.43% and a return per capital of 27.99%.
3. Brand Reputation: Tesla has established a strong brand reputation for its high-quality and environmentally friendly products, attracting a loyal customer base.
4. Technological Advancements: Tesla's continuous focus on technological advancements gives it a competitive edge in the industry.
Weaknesses
1. Competition: The electric vehicle industry is becoming increasingly competitive, with several established players and new entrants vying for market share. Tesla faces competition from both traditional automakers and new electric vehicle startups.
2. Production Capacity: Tesla has faced challenges in meeting the high demand for its vehicles due to production capacity constraints. This has led to delays in deliveries and potential loss of customers.
Opportunities
1. Growing Electric Vehicle Market: The global shift towards sustainable transportation presents a significant opportunity for Tesla to expand its market share and increase sales.
2. Expansion into International Markets: Tesla has the opportunity to further expand its presence in international markets, particularly in countries with favorable government policies and incentives for electric vehicles.
3. Energy Storage Solutions: The increasing demand for renewable energy and energy storage systems presents an opportunity for Tesla to further capitalize on its energy generation and storage segment.
Threats
1. Regulatory Environment: Changes in government regulations and policies related to electric vehicles and renewable energy could impact Tesla's operations and profitability.
2. Supply Chain Disruptions: Tesla relies on a complex global supply chain, and any disruptions or shortages of key components could impact production and delivery timelines.
3. Price Volatility: The price of raw materials, particularly lithium-ion batteries, can be volatile, which may impact Tesla's production costs and profitability.
In conclusion, Tesla, Inc. has strong profitability, backed by impressive net income, earnings per share, and EBITDA figures. The company's strengths lie in its innovative products, strong financial performance, brand reputation, and technological advancements. However, it also faces challenges such as intense competition, production capacity constraints, and potential regulatory and supply chain risks.
